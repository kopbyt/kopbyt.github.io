Here, we present women who contributed to the field of applied mathematics.

  1. Karen Aardal (born 1961), Norwegian and Dutch applied mathematician, theoretical computer scientist, and operations researcher
  2. Tatyana Afanasyeva (1876–1964), Russian-Dutch researcher in statistical mechanics, randomness, and geometry education
  3. Amandine Aftalion (born 1973), French applied mathematician, studies superfluids and the mathematics of footracing
  4. Ilka Agricola (born 1973), German expert on differential geometry and its applications in mathematical physics
  5. Dorit Aharonov (born 1970), Israeli specialist in quantum computing
  6. Beatrice Aitchison (1908–1997), American topologist who became a transportation economist in the US civil service
  7. Asuman Aksoy, Turkish-American functional analyst
  8. Stephanie B. Alexander, American differential geometer
  9. Linda J. S. Allen, American mathematician and mathematical biologist
  10. Elizabeth S. Allman (born 1965), American mathematical biologist
  11. Ann S. Almgren, American applied mathematician who works on computational simulations of supernovae and white dwarfs
  12. Kathleen Antonelli (1921–2006), Irish-American programmer of ENIAC, the first general-purpose electronic digital computer
  13. Elayne Arrington, American aerospace engineer, expert on Soviet aircraft
  14. Winifred Asprey (1917–2007), helped establish the first computer science lab at Vassar
  15. Tamara Awerbuch-Friedlander, American biomathematician and public health scientist
  16. Hertha Ayrton (1854–1923), English engineer, mathematician, physicist, and inventor, winner of the Hughes Medal
