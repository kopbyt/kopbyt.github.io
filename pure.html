Here, we present women who contributed to the field of pure mathematics.

  1. Maria Gaetana Agnesi wrote one of the first calculus textbooks in 1748. She was offered a professorship by the Bologna Academy of Sciences, making her the first female mathematics professor since antiquity, but it is unknown whether she accepted.[4]
  2. Hanan Mohamed Abdelrahman, Egyptian and Norwegian mathematics educator
  3. Izabela Abramowicz (1889–1973), Polish mathematician and mathematics educator
  4. Louise Doris Adams (died 1965), British mathematics reformer, president of the Mathematical Association
  5. Rachel Blodgett Adams (1894–1982), American mathematician, one of the earliest mathematics doctorates from Radcliffe College
  6. Maria Gaetana Agnesi (1718–1799), Italian mathematician and philosopher, possibly the first female mathematics professor
  7. Nkechi Agwu (born 1962), African American ethnomathematician
  8. Meike Akveld, Swiss knot theorist and mathematics educator
  9. Mara Alagic, Serbian mathematics educator, editor-in-chief of Journal of Mathematics and the Arts
  10. Lara Alcock, British mathematics educator and author
  11. Helen Popova Alderson (1924–1972), Russian and British mathematician and translator, wrote on quasigroups and reciprocity laws
  12. Grace Alele-Williams (born 1932), first woman to lead a Nigerian university
  13. Aldona Aleškevičienė-Statulevičienė (1936–2017), Lithuanian probability theorist
  14. Florence Eliza Allen (1876–1960), second female and fourth overall mathematics PhD from the University of Wisconsin
  15. Melania Alvarez, Mexican-Canadian mathematics educator, organizer of summer mathematics camps for indigenous students
  16. Yvette Amice (1936–1993), French expert on p-adic analysis who became president of the French mathematical society
  17. Divsha Amirà (1899–1966), Israeli geometer and mathematics educator
  18. T. A. Sarasvati Amma (1918–2000), Historian of ancient Indian mathematics
  19. Astrid an Huef, New Zealand expert on functional analysis, president of New Zealand Mathematical Society
  20. Nalini Anantharaman (born 1976), French mathematical physicist, winner of the Henri Poincaré Prize
  21. Beverly Anderson (born 1943), American mathematician, director of minority programs for Mathematical Sciences Education Board
  22. Kirsti Andersen (born 1941), Danish historian of mathematics
  23. Andreian Cazacu (1928–2018), Romanian complex analyst
  24. Hajnal Andréka (born 1947), Hungarian researcher in algebraic logic
  25. Annie Dale Biddle Andrews (1885–1940), algebraic geometer, first female PhD from the University of California, Berkeley
  26. Grace Andrews (mathematician) (1869–1951), one of only two women listed in the first edition of American Men of Science
